
# Founding Host Badge — Purpose, Design & Long-Term Value

## What a Founding Host Is

A **Founding Host** is a host who:

- Listed a property during the platform’s early launch phase (Currently a 3 month long stage)
- Took on higher uncertainty in exchange for helping establish the marketplace
- Contributed to the early quality, credibility, and culture of the platform   

This is **not a marketing badge** — it’s a recognition of early trust and participation. We aren't trying to gameify this. It's a simple reward mechanism.


## Why the Founding Host Concept Matters to Us, and to Them

### 1. Trust Signalling to Guests

For early guests, especially in a niche like horse travel:

- Platform trust is fragile
- Guests rely on signals beyond reviews

A Founding Host badge:

- Indicates early vetting and commitment (We can keep a closer eye on people at the start, when traffic is low. This allows us to build higher content)
- Suggests higher engagement and responsiveness
- Helps guests choose confidently when reviews are still limited

**Key insight:**  
When reviews are scarce, _proven participation_ becomes the trust signal.


### 2. Psychological Ownership for Hosts

Founding Hosts feel:

- Invested in the platform’s success (They can grow with it)
- Seen and valued as contributors, not inventory (Community aspect)
- More likely to give feedback and advocate for the platform (Improvements)

This creates:

- Lower churn
- Higher-quality listings
- Better early standards

**This turns hosts into partners, not users.**


## How the Badge Should Be Presented

### Visual Treatment

- Subtle, tasteful badge (e.g. “Founding Host”)
- Shown on:
    - Listing cards
    - Host profile
    - Search results (non-dominant)

**Never flashy. Never salesy.** - Always understated. Others need not even know about it. Might not need to even be much of a badge! But its shown, and they can feel proud about it.


### Language & Tone

Use heritage-style language:

- “Founding Host”
- “Early Contributor”
- “With us from the start”

Avoid:

- “VIP”
- “Exclusive”
- Anything implying superiority over later hosts

We aren't looking to divide people. There are no obvious tiers of customers (Despite backend programming saying otherwise).

## Practical Benefits (Soft, Non-Binding)

The power of the Founding Host program comes from **suggested value**, not hard promises.

Examples:

### 1. Fee Advantages (Optional & Revisit)

- Extended or permanent reduced host fee (-0.5%)
- Occasional fee-free promotions
- Priority access to future tools

**Important:**  
Phrase these as _possible future benefits_, not guarantees.

> “Founding Hosts may receive access to future benefits as the platform evolves.”

This keeps doors open without legal or operational lock-in. It also intrigues. Basically the mystery loot box of the staycation world.


### 2. Platform Reciprocity

Subtle reciprocal advantages:

- Discount when Founding Hosts book stays themselves (-5% our fee as they make us money anyway.)
- Early access to new features
- Ability to influence roadmap decisions (Like being on a discord, they have priority over being heard on website improvements)

This reinforces:

- Community loop
- Host ↔ Guest duality (many hosts will also travel)


### 3. Cultural Signalling

Founding Hosts set the tone:

- Better listing quality
- More thoughtful pricing
- Stronger engagement with routes and community tools

This helps establish:

- Platform norms
- What “good hosting” looks like in this niche


## How It Fits the Broader Platform Philosophy

The Founding Host badge aligns with your core principles:

- **We exist to serve an underserved community**
- **Power sits with hosts**
- **Trust and transparency over growth-at-all-costs**
- **Community before scale**

It also solves early-stage problems:

- Lack of reviews
- Low marketplace density
- Trust gap in niche travel


## Guardrails (What It Is NOT)

To avoid resentment or imbalance:

- Founding Host ≠ permanent ranking boost
- Founding Host ≠ algorithmic favoritism
- Founding Host ≠ price advantage over others

It is:  
✔ Recognition  
✔ Trust signal  
✔ Relationship marker

Not:  
✘ A hierarchy  
✘ A lock-in mechanism  
✘ A forever perk without reassessment


## Sunset & Legacy Consideration

The Founding Host program should:

- Be time-bound (e.g. first X months or first X hosts)
- Be permanently visible on those hosts’ profiles
- Never re-opened once closed (Early adopters ONLY)

This preserves its meaning and avoids dilution.


## One-Line Summary

> **Founding Hosts are recognised not for being better — but for being there first. They are our first supporters**.
