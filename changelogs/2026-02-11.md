### 2. Card-Based Review Flow ("I've ridden this route!")

**File:** `components/routes/route-detail-drawer.tsx`

A 5-step linear review flow designed to maximise completion and respect users' time:

- **Step 1 - Overall Feel**: Star rating (collected but NOT publicly displayed). Framed as "How did this route ride overall?"
- **Step 2 - Add a Moment**: Optional photo upload (placeholder UI). Prompted as sharing a memory.
- **Step 3 - Things Worth Knowing**: Checkbox tags (12 options) including Muddy after rain, Road crossings, Suitable for steady/experienced horses, Water available, Group friendly, Parking nearby, Good waymarking, Great views, Gates to open, Steep sections, Good surface. These produce structured, reusable data.
- **Step 4 - Quick Note**: Optional one-line note ("Anything you'd tell a friend before riding this?") with 200-character hard limit.
- **Step 5 - Thank You**: Review submitted. Optional long-form note framed as "Anything else you'd like to note?" with clear skip button. Long text is treated as context, not signal.

Progress bar shown throughout. Skip buttons on every step. Designed to feel finite and avoid review fatigue.

### 3. X Button Dismiss Behavior Fix

**Files:** `components/routes/route-detail-drawer.tsx`, `app/routes/page.tsx`

## Fix: Modal Scrolling & Full-Bleed Photos

### Session Summary

Fixed two bugs in the route detail modal: content was not scrollable (couldn't see sections below the fold), and photos had a white gap on either side instead of spanning the full card width.

### Changes

**File:** `components/routes/route-detail-drawer.tsx`

1. **Scrolling fix**: Replaced Radix `ScrollArea` wrapper around `drawerContent` with a native `<div className="h-full overflow-y-auto">`. The Radix ScrollArea was not receiving a proper height constraint from the flex layout, preventing scroll on both desktop (mouse wheel) and mobile (touch).
2. **Photo width fix**: Added `min-h-0` to the flex child wrapper (`<div className="flex-1 min-h-0">`) around the scrollable content. Removed `overflow-hidden` from this wrapper which was clipping the Radix ScrollArea's internal scroll mechanism. The Radix ScrollArea Viewport also reserved scrollbar gutter space on Windows, causing photos to not reach the card edges — switching to native scroll eliminates this.

### Risk Notes

- The Radix ScrollArea is still used for the discussion panel and waypoints panel (separate from the main content scroll). Only the outer content wrapper was changed.
- Native `overflow-y-auto` provides consistent scroll behavior across all platforms.

### Verification Steps

1. Open a route modal — scroll down with mouse wheel or touch — all content sections should be reachable
2. Photos at the top of the modal should span the full width with no white gaps on the sides
3. Run `npm run build` — no errors
