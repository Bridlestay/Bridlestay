# Changelog – 2026-02-09

## 🗺️ New Route Pin Click Behavior (PC & Mobile)

### What Changed

The entire flow for clicking route pins on the map has been redesigned to match OS Maps' approach, using Padoq's white and green theme.

### 1. Route Quick Card (New Component)

**File:** `components/routes/route-quick-card.tsx`

- **What it does:** When a user clicks a route pin on the map, instead of a popup card appearing on the pin itself, the route is now drawn directly on the map and a small, elegant card slides up from the bottom of the screen.
- **What it shows:** Route name, author (with avatar), distance, estimated ride time, difficulty badge, and number of completions. No star rating.
- **How to use:** Click any route pin on the map. The route polyline appears instantly, and the quick card animates in from the bottom. Click the quick card to see full details. Click the X button to dismiss it and clear the route.
- **Design:** White card with rounded corners, subtle shadow, green accent "Tap for full details" bar at the bottom. Responsive for both mobile and desktop.

### 2. Centered Route Detail Modal (Redesigned)

**File:** `components/routes/route-detail-drawer.tsx`

- **What changed:** The route detail panel has been completely redesigned from a side-panel/drawer layout to a centered modal card with a blurred backdrop.
- **How it works:** When the quick card is clicked (or a route is opened from Find Routes/Saved Routes), a large informative card animates into the center of the screen. The map behind it is blurred for focus.
- **Features:**
  - Green accent header bar with route title
  - All existing route information (stats, description, photos, discussion, waypoints, hazards, nearby stays, etc.) preserved
  - Bottom action bar with Like, Save, and Share buttons
  - Smooth zoom-in animation (scale + slide from bottom)
  - Click the blurred backdrop or the X button to close
- **Design:** White and green Padoq theme. Max width 672px (2xl), max height 85vh. Rounded corners, clean shadow.

### 3. Map Pin Click Simplified

**File:** `components/routes/routes-map-mapbox.tsx`

- **What changed:** Removed the popup card that previously appeared on top of the pin when clicked. The pin click now directly:
  1. Draws the route polyline on the map
  2. Zooms the camera to fit the route
  3. Triggers the quick card at the bottom of the screen
- **Why:** The old popup card required a two-step process (click pin → see popup → click "View details"). The new flow is faster and more intuitive: click pin → see route + quick card → click card for full details.
- **Cleanup:** Removed the `getMapboxThumbnailUrl` import (no longer needed in map component), removed the custom `route-click` window event listener, and removed all popup HTML generation code.

### 4. Page Integration

**File:** `app/routes/page.tsx`

- **New state:** `previewRoute` tracks the currently previewed route for the quick card.
- **`handleRoutePreview`:** Now draws the route polyline on the map immediately, zooms to fit, and shows the quick card.
- **`handleRouteClick`:** Opens the full centered modal with all route details.
- **`handleClosePreview`:** Dismisses the quick card and clears the route from the map.
- **Tab switching:** Changing tabs automatically closes any open quick card or modal.

## Summary of User-Facing Changes

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| Pin click → popup card on pin → "View details" → side panel | Pin click → route drawn + bottom card → tap card → centered modal |
| Two clicks to see details | One click to see route, one more for details |
| Side panel (left drawer) | Centered modal with blur backdrop |
| Popup card with HTML injection | Clean React component (RouteQuickCard) |
| Desktop-only side panel + separate mobile slide-up | Same centered modal on both PC and mobile |

## How to Test

1. Go to the Routes page
2. Click any route pin on the map
3. The route should draw immediately and a small card should appear at the bottom
4. Click the small card to see the full route details in a centered modal
5. Click the blurred backdrop or X button to close the modal
6. Opening routes from Find Routes or Saved Routes panels also uses the new modal
7. Test on both desktop and mobile viewports
